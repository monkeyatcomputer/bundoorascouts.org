## The World's Largest Youth Movement

Scouting was founded in 1907 by Robert Baden-Powell and today reaches **57 million young people** across 170 countries. In Australia, Scouting is delivered by [Scouts Australia](https://scouts.com.au) through state branches like [Scouts Victoria](https://scoutsvictoria.com.au).

## The Scouting Method

Scouting isn't just camping — it's a holistic development framework built around:

### Learning by Doing
Scouts learn through experience. Rather than sitting in classrooms, they build fires, navigate by compass, and solve real problems in real environments.

### The Patrol System
Young people work in small, self-governing groups (Patrols) — learning leadership, accountability, and teamwork in a natural social setting.

### The Promise & Law
The Scout Promise and Scout Law provide a moral and ethical framework that guides behaviour in all areas of life, not just Scouting.

### Progressive Challenges
From simple knots at age 5 to international expeditions at 18, the program grows with the young person — always challenging, never overwhelming.

## Why Scouting Works

Research consistently shows that participation in Scouting:

- Builds resilience, problem-solving, and adaptability
- Develops leadership and public speaking confidence
- Fosters respect for the environment
- Creates lasting friendships across diverse backgrounds
- Provides skills employers actively look for

## STEM in Scouting

Modern Scouting embraces technology and STEM education alongside traditional outdoor skills. Cubs and Scouts regularly engage with coding, robotics, and environmental science.
